abstract: It has long been recognized that self-shrinkers serve as models for Type I singularities of the mean curvature flow, and their classification remains a problem to which considerable effort has been devoted. The present work concerns itself with graphical hypersurfaces whose study reduces, as we shall recall, to the analysis of a quasilinear elliptic partial differential equation. The Dirichlet problem for this equation asks of us to find a function satisfying the self-shrinker equation throughout and a Dirchlet boundary condition on the boundary of the region simultaneously. It is not apparent in reviewing the literature that the question of uniqueness for this problem has been addressed directly. We therefore show here that the Dirichlet problem for graphical self-shrinkers admits at most one solution. The argument proceeds by linearization, after which a maximum principle constrains the difference between any two solutions to vanish identically.
